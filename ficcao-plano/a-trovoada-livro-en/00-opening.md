## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Tranquilina — the quiet one — daughter of people of the village, born on a night of thunderstorm, and the name was a promise and an irony, of the kind life plays on houses without their noticing: the mother, who lived with the father a love of shouts and of reconciliations, of partings and returns, of storm upon storm, set on her daughter, born in the middle of a storm, the name of the quiet one, begging of the word the calm the house did not have; and the girl grew up with the name of calm and the heart of the storm, because what is learned in a house is stronger than what is promised at a christening.

The register says who was born, on what night, of whom, and who married whom. It does not say why there are hearts that learned to read love by the din, nor what it costs, to one who learned it so, to tell, already grown, the calm from the desert. For that, books such as this one are needed.
