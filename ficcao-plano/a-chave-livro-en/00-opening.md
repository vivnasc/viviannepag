## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Preciosa — precious, the one of worth — daughter of the village, and the name was prophecy and trap: they called her Preciosa, the one who is worth much, the one of price, and she fulfilled the name by making herself indispensable, the one who cannot be done without, the one without whom nothing moves; but the name, which seemed a blessing, was a trap, because whoever makes herself indispensable becomes bound to the very thing that makes her indispensable, and Preciosa made herself the key of a mill.

There are, in the register of Véspera, names that appear bound to a place as though they were of it: Preciosa appears bound to the Spinning Mill, the mill of spinning, for thirty years, the one who opens before all and closes after all, the key; and so bound that no one thinks them apart any more, Preciosa and the Mill, as though the mill were hers and she were of the mill, and the book would barely know how to write the one without the other.

The register keeps who holds the houses and the trades of the village, and to what thing each name fastened itself. It does not say who a person is without what she does, nor what is discovered, on the far side of the fear, on the day one hands over the key and sees the mill go on without us. For that, books such as this one are needed.
