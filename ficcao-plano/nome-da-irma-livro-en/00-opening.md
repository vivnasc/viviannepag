## From the register of Véspera

In the record of the village, in the hand of the Keepers who have kept the book, the same name is found entered twice, in the same writing.

Eufémia, daughter of Bendito and of Saudade. Born in the spring, dead in the spring, nineteen years between the one and the other. She was about to take her course in the City when the fever carried her off in nine days.

Eufémia, daughter of Bendito and of Saudade. Born in the winter when the river took the Bridge, nine months after the first was laid in the earth. The name was a promise: at the graveside, the mother put her hand on her belly and swore that the name would not die there.

The register says who was born and who died. It does not say who was left with whose life. For that, books such as this one are needed.
