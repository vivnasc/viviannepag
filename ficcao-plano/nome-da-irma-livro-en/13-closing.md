## From the register of Véspera

In the record of the village, in the hand of the Keeper of this time, there was entered this year:

Saudade, widow of Bendito. Mother of Eufémia, who died at nineteen years and is on the eastern slope. And mother of Eufémia, who lives in the house facing the square. In this order, which was their order.

And on the middle shelf, between the book of the births and that of the deaths of fifty years ago, there is now kept also a brown notebook, of worn corners, with a round writing on the cover. It is neither an entry nor a paper of law. It stays where the names stay: in sight, in order, beside her own.

The entries say who was born and who died. They do not say who lived, nor when they began to live, which is not always on the same day. There was this year in Véspera a woman who began. She was known by her writing.

And of all of it a record was made.
