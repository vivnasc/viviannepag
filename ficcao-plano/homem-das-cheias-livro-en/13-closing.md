## From the register of Véspera

The Keeper of this time says that there was a winter, in the village, when a woman ceased to go to the window.

Rosário, daughter of Vado and of Lembrança, who came from four generations of women who loved men who went away, and who was the first of her house to turn from the window to the room, and to choose, with her eyes open, the bank where one stays instead of the bank one dreams.

The register says that she married, and to whom, and when, and that stays written here, like everything that can be written. The rest, what does not fit in a book — that she learned to love what was there, that she traded the window for the Bridge, that she taught her heart, late but in time, that one does not have to run after the water —, that stays written in another place, in the one place where the things that matter are recorded: in the life of whoever comes after, who is no longer going to learn, in that house, to look at the road.

And of all of it a record was made.
