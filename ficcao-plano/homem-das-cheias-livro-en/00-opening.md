## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Rosário, daughter of Vado and of Lembrança, born on the near bank, on the side of the village, in a winter of great waters.

The father, Vado, a man of fairs, who crossed the world buying and selling and came back with the seasons, and who is set down in this book as married and as absent, which was what he was most.

The mother, Lembrança, who waited for him at the window her whole life, and who of the waiting made her trade, and of the trade her fate.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who departed and when. It does not say who was loved from near and who was loved from afar, nor why there are hearts that only know how to kindle with the door closing. For that, books like this one are needed.
