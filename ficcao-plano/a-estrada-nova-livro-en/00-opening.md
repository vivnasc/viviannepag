## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Aurora, daughter of Firmino and of Véspera folk. She was the first at everything: the best of the village school, the first of her house to rise, the first woman of Véspera to reach places no woman had reached. She rose her whole life, step by step, without stopping, and was the pride of the family and of the village.

The name was a promise, of the Véspera kind: her mother wanted her the name of the dawn, of the breaking of the day, of a luminous beginning, dreaming of a daughter who would rise and go far. And life gave a woman who was always in the dawn of a new goal and never in the noon of having arrived, because each victory, in the instant she reached it, became the step of the next, and the arrival she pursued always drew back, one more step, one more new road.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who departed. It does not say what it costs one who runs a whole life after an arrival that always draws back, nor what one pursues, without seeing it, that after all may not be ours, nor where one stops, if one stops at all, who has climbed every stair and never felt they arrived. For that, books such as this one are needed.
