## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, one looks for Plácida and almost does not find her, so little did she leave written.

Plácida, daughter of people of the village, born at the end of a full house, the youngest of many. Married to Brando, of the fields, a meek man, widow of him for some years. Mother of Belmira. Grandmother of Bento.

And nothing more. A whole life that scarcely touched the paper, because Plácida made a point of touching nothing, and managed it, and it was that her talent and her fate: to pass without weighing, to fit in any corner, to give no one any trouble, not even the register, which always held her easy to write, so little did she ask for herself.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who departed. It does not say who was seen and who spent their life making a point of not being, nor what it costs, a person, the talent of not bothering. For that, books like this one are needed.
