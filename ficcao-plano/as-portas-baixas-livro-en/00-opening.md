## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Modesta, daughter of Augusta and of Véspera folk. She had the best voice the choir of the village ever knew, and she sang it her whole life inward, hidden, leaving the solos to others, smothering what she had of best so as not to be noticed.

The name was a promise: her mother wanted her the name of modesty, dreaming of a daughter demure, who would not stand out, because in the village and in her time to stand out, and even more in a woman, was a thing that was feared. And life fulfilled it beyond the dream: it gave a woman who made herself so small that she almost disappeared, who spent her life stooped like one who passes through low doors, without ever measuring her true height.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who departed. It does not say what it costs one who has a gift and buries it out of fear of standing out, nor whether the hour comes, and how, when a voice kept inward a whole lifetime is at last let loose. For that, books such as this one are needed.
