## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Dores, daughter of Véspera folk, widow of Aniceto, mother of Joaquim. She spent her life saving those who were sinking: first the husband, who drank, then the son, who inherited from his father the thirst and the shadow. She held the house alone at the cost of wearing herself away, because she had learned, as a girl, that to love was that, was to save.

Her name was a promise, of the old and hard ones: her mother wanted her the name of the sorrows, in the hope of a daughter with a back to bear them. And life gave a woman who bore the sorrows of everyone and never her own, until the night when she had to choose between her son and herself.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who departed. It does not say what it costs a mother to set a limit to a son she loves, nor whether there is, from the depth of so much sheltering, a door that may still be shut to the harm without being shut to the one who brings it. For that, books such as this one are needed.
