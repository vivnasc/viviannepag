## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Eulália, daughter of Véspera folk, mother of Tomé. She was, for a whole lifetime, her son's voice to the world: the one who translated him to the doctors, to the teachers, to the village, because he had come into the world saying things in another way, and it was she, and she alone, who learned his language.

Her name was a promise: her mother wanted her the name of a speech easy and clear, dreaming of a daughter who would know how to say herself to the world. And life gave a woman who spent her life saying others, and who, very late, barely remembering the sound of her own voice, came to wonder whether she had one too.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who departed. It does not say what it costs a mother to be the only bridge between a son and the world, nor what falls silent beneath a borrowed voice until one's own no longer knows its own sound. For that, books such as this one are needed.
