## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Esperança — hope — daughter of people of the village, born in the spring, and the name was a promise, like almost all those of Véspera, but this one was of a particular kind: the mother, who had lived a whole life awaiting the life her husband was going to give her next year, set on her daughter the name of Hope not to bless her, though she thought she did, but because hope was the one thing the house had always had in abundance, and what a house has in abundance is what it passes to its daughters, even when it is what most harms them.

The register says who married whom, and when, and keeps, without meaning to, the dates of a beginning without the date of an end. It does not say how much of a life is walled up in an unfinished house, awaiting a roof that is going to come next year. For that, books such as this one are needed.
