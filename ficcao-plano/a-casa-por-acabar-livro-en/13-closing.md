## From the register of Véspera

And of everything a record was made.

Be it set down that Esperança, daughter of people of the village, who had been the betrothed of Venturoso of the house on the slope for twenty years, stopped awaiting the house to be finished, and broke off the engagement, and gave, at last, the first use to her own life, in the house she had and with the time that remained to her. Be it set down that she climbed, one last time, to the unfinished house, and saw it in the sun, beautiful as a skeleton, and asked herself how much of her life was walled up there, and that she came down the slope resolved to wall up nothing more, to live the present, which is the only house one truly inhabits.

The register keeps the beginnings and the ends, and by them one reads, in the dates that do not close, the lives put on hold. This Keeper saw, in the book, Esperança's engagement last twenty years without becoming a marriage, and saw, pages back, her mother await a husband who was going to, and die waiting; and understood that the unfinished house was not unfinished, it was finished at not being finished, and that the engagement of twenty years was not the waiting for a life, it already was the life, and that Esperança, in stopping waiting, did not lose a house nor a man: she recovered a life, her own, that had been immured for twenty years in some walls in the sun.

Whoever reads this entry and recognises herself in some Esperança, who loves what someone promises to be and does not see what they are, who postpones her life awaiting the other to finish the house, change, arrive, be at last ready, let her know what she learned and what this house records: that one loves a person for what they are, not for what they are to be; that a house not finished in twenty years perhaps will never be finished, because there are men whose whole work is the unfinishing; and that life is not the house to come, it is the day one has, and that to postpone it awaiting a better one is the most beautiful and the saddest way of not living it.

Esperança brought her heart from the future to the present, from the promise to the real, and inhabited, at last, the house that had always been hers, which was not the one on the slope, it was the life of today. And the unfinished house stayed on the slope, in the sun, beautiful and empty, without the life of any woman immured in its walls, because the one who had been walled there twenty years went out, at last, into the sun, and came down to live, which is what this village, book by book, is slowly learning: that one does not await life; one lives it, today, in the house there is.

And of everything a record was made, by the hand of the Keeper, in the register of Véspera.
