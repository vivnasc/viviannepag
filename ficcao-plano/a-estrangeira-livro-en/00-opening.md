## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Peregrina — the pilgrim — daughter of people who left, born between one land and another, and the name was a prophecy: the mother, who emigrated young and had her daughter already abroad, gave her the name of one who is always on the way, of one who wanders, without knowing that she was setting in her daughter's cradle the fate of having her feet in one land and her heart in another and her home in neither, which is what happens to the children of emigration, who are born foreigners on both sides, of here for having been born there, of there for being of here.

The register keeps those who arrive and those who leave, and in which column each name is written. It does not say to which land one belongs who was born between two, nor where the home lies of one who has it divided across banks the river keeps apart. For that, books such as this one are needed.
