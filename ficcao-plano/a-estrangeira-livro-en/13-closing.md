## From the register of Véspera

And of everything a record was made.

Be it set down that Peregrina, daughter of people who left, born between one land and another, returned to her parents' village convinced that she was returning to her land, and discovered that she was of neither here nor there, that she had two homes and none; and that, instead of going on looking for the land she belonged to by origin, she learned the hardest thing of one who is from nowhere: that belonging is not inherited, it is chosen, and that the home one looks for abroad is built first within, and then, brick by brick, in the land where one decides to stay. Be it set down that she stayed, and planted herself, and took root by decision, and made of the village her home, not by having been born in it, but by having chosen it.

The register keeps those who arrive and those who leave, and there are names that appear split down the middle, with two addresses and neither whole. This Keeper showed Peregrina, in the book, that half the village had entries so, of people who left and returned, and that she was not alone in her pain; and saw, afterwards, over the years, Peregrina's entry change column, leave that of those who are passing through and pass to that of those who stayed, not because her origin changed, for that does not change, but because her course began to run in the village, like the water of the fountain, which comes from away and becomes of here by running here. And the Keeper understood that Peregrina had fulfilled the name the opposite of how it seemed: she pilgrimed her whole life in search of a land, and the pilgrimage ended not when she found the land she belonged to, but when she decided to belong to the land where she was.

Whoever reads this entry and recognises herself in some Peregrina who belongs nowhere, whose heart is divided between two lands and whole in neither, who always hides one half to fit, who lives with the bag half-packed ready to leave at the first pain, let her know what she learned and what this house records: that belonging does not come from where one is born, it is made where one stays; that home is in no land waiting, it is built, within first and then in a street; that to have two origins is not to have half of each, it is to have two belongings, and it is a wealth and not a lack; and that the greater courage, for one who is from nowhere, is not to go on looking for the right land, it is to choose one, and to set down the bag, and to plant oneself, and to cross in it the disappointments without fleeing, until one discovers, with astonishment, that one belongs not to where one is born, but to where one takes root.

Peregrina traded the search for the choice, the half-packed bag for the roots, the two lands she was not of for the one land she made hers. And in the streets of Véspera, since the one in which a cousin introduced her as of the village, there is one more woman who no longer feels herself a foreigner everywhere, because she learned that the land one belongs to is the one one decides to love, and that to take root is a decision, and not an origin, and that one who learns to make a home by choice can be at home in any land to which she gives herself.

And of everything a record was made, by the hand of the Keeper, in the register of Véspera.
