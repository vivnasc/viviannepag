## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Fartura — plenty — daughter of people of the village, born in the second of the years of hunger, when the mountain gave nothing and the river brought nothing and in Véspera the mouths were counted as coins are counted, slowly and with fear.

The name was a promise, one of the most anxious the village ever set down: the mother, still carrying her, with the house living on roots and on faith, wanted the daughter to bring in her name the thing that was missing from the table, and called her Plenty, so that the word might do what the earth would not, and the girl might have, at least at her christening, what the house did not have in its chest.

The register says who was born, in which house, with which name. It does not say why there are those who come into plenty and never taste it, nor what chest that is which is filled a whole life and never opened, nor what a person keeps in the pantry when what she keeps there is fear. For that, books such as this one are needed.
