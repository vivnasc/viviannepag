## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Benvinda, of the house of the Grocery. Daughter of Abílio, the grocer, who inherited the shop and the ledger, and of the shop and the ledger made her house and her sentence.

The name was a promise, like almost all of them: she was born in a year of good harvests, with the larder full and the door open, and the father wanted the name to say what he felt at the coming of everyone. Benvinda. Welcome you be.

The register says who was born, who married, who departed. It does not say who owes whom, nor what it costs one who pays, nor what is silenced by one who forgives. For that there are other ledgers, and this is one of them.
