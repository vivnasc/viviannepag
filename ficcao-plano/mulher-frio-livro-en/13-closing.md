## From the register of Véspera

The Keeper of this time says that there was a winter, in the houses of the height, when the woman who was never cold learned, at last, to feel it.

Serafina, of the Serranos, who was orphaned in the Great Winter and raised the siblings at eleven years old, and who held the house of the height for sixty-three years without ever weeping, without ever being cold, without ever letting them care for her. And who, one winter of snow, with a granddaughter keeping her company, at last let the hearth be lit for her, let the blanket be put on her knees, and wept the parents the snow had taken, before a grave she had never had. And who said, by the hearth, at the end of a life: after all I was always cold.

There was added also, that year, in the cemetery of the serra, a stone with the names of two whom the snow took and never gave back, so that their own, at the end of sixty-three years, might have at last somewhere to weep them.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who was lost in the snow. It does not say what it costs a girl to freeze everything she feels to save her siblings, nor the warmth that is needed, and for how long, for a woman to feel again the cold she always had. For that, books like this one are needed. And of all of it a record was made.
