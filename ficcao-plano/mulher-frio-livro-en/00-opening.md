## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, in the part of the houses of the height, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Serafina, of the Serranos, of the house of the height. Daughter of parents who were lost on the serra in the Great Winter, and who are set down in this book with a line of death and no grave, because the snow never gave them back.

She was eleven years old in the winter when she was left alone with the small siblings. She raised them, married them off, buried those who went, and held the house of the height for sixty years, and in sixty years no one of the village saw her weep, nor be cold, nor need anything. For that they called her, and call her, the woman who was never cold.

The register says who was born, who married whom, who was lost in the snow. It does not say what it costs a girl of eleven to freeze everything she feels so that they do not all die, nor how long that ice takes to melt, nor that it takes, sometimes, a granddaughter and a whole winter for a woman to feel again the cold she always had. For that, books like this one are needed.
