## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Perpétua — the lasting one — daughter of the House of the Mill, the cousin who was left with the mother-house, and the name was a promise, of the kind that are kept the wrong way round.

The mother wanted her daughter to be lasting, to endure, to hold; and the daughter kept it in a way the mother did not foresee, holding the whole family on her back her whole life, as a main beam holds a roof, without anyone, in fifty years, asking the beam how it was.

The register says who was left with whose house, who sustained the feasts, who ran to whom. It does not say what weight it is that is not seen because it looks like strength, nor what creaks inside a beam the whole house forgets is also timber, nor what a person asks herself when, after a life of holding everything, she is holding nothing. For that, books such as this one are needed.
