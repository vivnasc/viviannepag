## 9. The relapse

But one does not lower a wall of twenty years without its rising again at the first thing, and Socorro had her relapse, and it came in the form the relapses of those who learned not to receive always come in: at the first sign that a dish could, in fact, turn against her, the wall rose all the way again, in an instant.

It came when one of the people who helped her, without malice, did or said a thing that touched the old wound. Perhaps she charged, lightly, for the care she had given; perhaps she commented to another on Socorro's illness, and the comment reached Socorro distorted; perhaps she only failed once, not appearing on a day on which Socorro already counted on her; and whatever it was, it was enough for Socorro to feel, again, with all the old force, the empty door, the confirmation that to accept is to risk being left, that the others' care comes with an invoice or is withdrawn, that she had been right, in twenty years, not to receive; and the wall, which she had begun to lower, rose all the way in an instant, and Socorro shut herself again, went back to returning the dishes full, went back to the it is not needed, I manage, went back to the smooth wall.

And it was terrible, because it was a real setback, and Socorro convinced herself that she was right, that she had seen well, that to accept had been a mistake, that that failure of one person proved that she should have stayed shut; and she generalised, again, from one failure to all people, from one dish that had disappointed her to all dishes, exactly as she had generalised, twenty years before, from the empty door to all doors; and she was about to shut herself for good again, to undo all she had learned, to confirm herself in the solitude, when it was the daughter, again, who showed it to her.

The daughter, on seeing the mother go back to returning the dishes, to shutting herself, understood the relapse, and said to her, with the affection of one who no longer hits against a wall but speaks with the mother: mother, someone failed you, and you shut yourself again to everyone. I know what happened. But look at what you are doing: because of one person who failed you now, are you going to return my dish to me again? Are you going to shut the door on me again, on me, who did not fail you? You are doing the same as twenty years ago, mother. One person fails you, and you punish everyone, you shut yourself to all, including those who never failed you. It is not just. I did not fail you. The stubborn neighbours did not fail you. Do not shut the door on us because of one who failed you. Distinguish, mother. There are those who fail and those who do not fail, and you cannot treat everyone as one who failed you, or you will be alone forever.

And Socorro, on hearing distinguish, mother, understood that the daughter was right, that she was doing the of-always, letting one failure erase all the non-failures, letting one empty door shut all doors; and she understood a deep thing about learning to receive: that it was not a thing one learns once and it stays, it was a thing one has to learn again each time someone fails, because there is always going to be someone who fails, and each failure makes the wall rise again, and what matters is not never again to be disappointed, for that is impossible, it is not to let one disappointment shut the door on those who did not disappoint, it is to distinguish those who fail from those who do not, and to go on receiving from those who do not fail, even after one who did. And she went back to lowering the wall, to accepting the dishes of the daughter and of the neighbours who did not fail her, and she understood that to have at her side one who would tell her distinguish, mother, do not shut the door on everyone because of one, was the difference between shutting herself forever at the first disappointment and learning to receive in spite of some disappointments, which is the only way to receive, because to receive without ever being disappointed does not exist, and what exists is to receive knowing that sometimes one is disappointed, and not letting that shut us to those who do not disappoint us.
