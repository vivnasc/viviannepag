## From the register of Véspera

In the register of the village, by the hand of the Keepers who passed through the book, is found:

Socorro — help, succour — daughter of the village, and the name was a wound set in baptism.

They called her Socorro, the one who comes to the rescue, and she fulfilled the name her whole life, being the help of others, the one who shelters, the one who is there when someone needs; but the name, which was for giving, turned against her, because the one who is the help of others learns to give and never to receive, and Socorro, who came to everyone's rescue, did not know, when misfortune struck her, how to let them come to hers.

The register keeps who gives and who receives in the houses of the village, and there are names that always appear on the side of those who come to the rescue, at births helping, at deaths shrouding, in illnesses watching, in troubles coming; and Socorro was one of those names, she was in the register of Véspera on the side of those who give, always, in fifty-five years, never on the side of those who receive.

The register keeps who gives and who receives, who comes to the rescue and to whom one comes. It does not say why there are those who return full the dishes left at their door, nor what year it was in which she counted on someone and was left looking at the empty door, nor what it costs, after that, to eat the first dish and return it empty, which in Véspera is the greatest declaration of trust there is. For that, books such as this one are needed.
